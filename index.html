Who is Yorushika? Everything you need to know

Yorushika is a Japanese music duo fromed in 2017, consisting of a vocalist and lyricist, n-buna, and the vocalist suis. The duo emerged as a fresh voice in the J-pop scene, quickly gaining attention for their compelling sound and narrative-driven lyrics.

Music Style

Their music blends J-pop with elements of rock and folk, often featuring intricate melodies and emotional lyrics Their songs often feature dynamic arrangements that incorporate strings, piano, and electric instruments, creating a layered listening experience. The duo’s style is marked by a strong emphasis on storytelling, where each track invites listeners to immerse themselves in their lyrical themes.

Albums

Yorushika has released two albums, Elma and Dakara Boku wa Ongaku wo Yameta, as well as several singles and EPs. Their debut album being “Elma,” released in 2018, received critical acclaim and established their presence in the Japanese music scene. It introduced listeners to their signature sound and narrative style, establishing them as a notable act in the Japanese music landscape. Their second album, Dakaboku,releases in 2020, deepens the narrative established in Elma. It explores the journey of Amy, with a more introspective and complex storytelling approach. The album has been praised for its lyrical depth and musical diversity, reinforcing their reputation as artists who push creative boundaries.

Themes

The duo’s lyrics frequently explore themes of love, loss, nostalgia and existential reflection, drawing listeners into deep emotional narratives. They often create a sense of longing and introspection, drawing listeners into their emotionally charged narratives. The duality of joy and sorrow in their songs resonates deeply with fans, making their music relatable and heartbreaking.

Pressence

Yorushika gained popularity through their music videos on YouTube, where they often share animated visuals that complement their songs. The visually stunning animations accompanying their songs not only enhance the storytelling but also create a captivating aesthetic that has become synonymous with their brand. Their online presence has helped cultivate a strong and dedicated fan community.

Collaborations

They have collaborated with various artists and producers, contributing to a diverse range of sounds within their discography. These collaborations often bring diverse influences into their work, allowing them to experiment with different genres and styles while maintaining their unique identity.

Fun Facts

The name Yorushika comes from 'Yoru shika mo nemurezu ni' (I can only sleep at night). N-buna and suis do not disclose their identity and have not revealed what they look like.

About n-buna

n-buna is a producer for vocaloid before he form the group Yorushika in 2017 with suis. He is known for his songs such as "Toumei Elegy" and "Umiyuri Kaiteitan" in his vocaloid days. Although he has not officially left the vocaloid community now he mostly produce music under Yorushika

Story of Elma and Amy

In two of the album, the story is about Amy (the protagonist of Dakaboku) going on a journey through Sweden, and then Elma (protagonist of Elma) taking the same journey one year later in Elma. Letters to Elma are a collection of letters and songs that are (in story) written by Amy, then later mailed in a box to Elma along with some photos. Elma’s diary is, well, just what it sounds like. All the songs are written by either Amy or Elma. Elma is told in straight chronological order, while Dakaboku is more ambiguous in its storytelling.
